Timely happenings in PDX

28th Annual Oregon Brewers Festival

Tom McCall Waterfront Park

The Festival starts on Wednesday, July 22 and goes through Sunday. Taps are open from noon to 9pm. We definitely recommend going on Wednesday or Thursday… the weekend gets crazy packed and you’ll have to spend all your time waiting in lines with empty beer cups!

It’s cash only; $7 for a glass and $1 per beer token. Everything’s sold separately, so you can share a glass between two people or buy your own, however you wanna make it happen… It’s 4 tokens to fill a glass and 1 token for a 3oz. sample.

oregonbrewfest.com

Recommended Sights & To-Do’s

International Rose Test Garden

SW Portland

The garden grounds are absolutely gorgeous, and feature over 10,000 rose bushes representing more than 650 rose varieties. If you’d like a free guided tour, they do them daily at 1pm, but freely walking the grounds is nice.

rosegardenstore.org

Kargi GoGo

Downtown Portland

Justin’s sister and brother-in-law, McKinze Cook and Sean Fredericks, own and run their Peace Corps inspired Georgian cart, serving food from the country of Georgia. Elements of Eastern European and Middle Eastern cuisines result in fresh, flavorful dishes.

kargigogo.com

Salt & Straw

Locations: Northwest, Alberta, and Division

It began with Kim & Tyler—part luck, part strategy and part love, in a custom push cart creating ice cream recipes. Now, their ice cream is legendary, and people wait in long lines to buy it. You do get all the free samples you want, before picking your flavor(s). We recommend going early, when they open, or whenever you pass by and the line isn’t outside the door.

saltandstraw.com
